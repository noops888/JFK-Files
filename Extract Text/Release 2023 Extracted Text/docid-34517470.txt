DOWNGRADING AND DECLASSIFICATION 015788
1. REFERENCE IS MADE TO CSCI-316/05215-67, 1 DECEMBER 1967,
ON THE PROB ABILITY THAT THE DESERTERS FROM USS INTREPID LIEFT JAPAN
ON THE SOVIET SHIP BAI KAL.
2. THE FOLLOWING RELATED INFORMATION WAS SUBSEQUENTLY PROVIDED
BY AN OFFICIAL OF A JAPANESE SECURITY SERVICE: ON 11 NOVEMBER 1967,
THE DAY OF THE BAIKAL 'S. DEPARTURE FROM JAPAN, JAPANESE POLICE CON7
DUCTING A SURVEILLANCE OPERATION PHOTOGRAPHED CARGO BEING LOADED
ON THE BAIKAL. IN THREE OF THESE PHOTO GRAPHS, CLEARLY VISIBLE ALONG
THE RAIL IN THE BACKGROUND, ARE THE SAME TWO PERSONS SHOWN ABROARD
THE BAIKAL IN AN "ASAHI GRAPH" PHOTOGRAPH PUBLISHED IN JAPAN ON 4
DECEMBER AND IDENTIFIED BY "ASAHI GRAPH" AS TWO OF THE DESERTERS
FROM THE USS INTREPID. THE POLICE PHOTOGRAPHS WERE FORWARDED
THROUGH JAPANESE GOVERNMENT CHANNELS TO THE JAPANESE FOREIGN
MINISTRY BY A POLICE OFFICAL WITH THE ORAL COMMENT THAT THIS WAS
CONSIDERED EVIDENCE THAT THE DESERTERS LEFT JAPAN ON THE BAIKAL.
THE POLICE DID NOT INFORM THE FOREIGN MINISTRY THAT THE PHOTO GRAPHS
HAD BEEN TAKEN IN A SURVEILLANCE OPERATION, BUT MERELY THAT THEY
"HAD COME INTO THE HANDS OF THE POLICE."
3. THE ABOVE INFORMATION IS BEING PASSED TO THE U. S. EMBASSY
IN TOKYO.
