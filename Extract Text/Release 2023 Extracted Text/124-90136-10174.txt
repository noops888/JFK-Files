This office requests clearance for passing to the French
Services the following information contained in a memorandum from
Cleveland, Ohio, dated 8 January 1959, subject and file number as
indicated above:
a.
"William Alexander MORGAN was born 19 April 1928 in
Cleveland, Ohio. His family moved to Toledo, Ohio when
he was one year old. He attended Rosary Cathedral School
and for two years was a student at Central Catholic High
School. He spent one year in the United States Merchant
Marines and then enlisted in the United States Army from
which he was dishonorably discharged. During 1957
MORGAN was employed by Munart Music Company in
Dayton, Ohio and at that time his residence was listed
as 99 Patterson Village Drive, Dayton, Ohio. He had
previously been employed as custodian at the Holy Rosary
Cathedral, Toledo, Ohio. He met the Cuban rebel leaders
in Miami, Florida. He was married to Theresa (nee name
given) in Miami, Florida in 1954. They have two
children. As of January 1959, his wife and children were
residing in Toledo, Ohio.
b. "According to MORGAN's father, William ran away from
his problems since he was a youngster. MORGAN senior
stated that in his opinion his son was emotionally disturbed
and believed he was in need of psychiatric help. In
December 1958 MORGAN senior stated that his son left
home on December 26, 1957, and he had not heard from
him or seen him since that time.
