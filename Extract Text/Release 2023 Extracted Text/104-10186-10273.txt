2. A usually reliable source, in a report dated 2 March 1962, reported
on the travel of Cubans through Rio de Janeiro during November
and December 1961. Included on the list was Ramiro RODRIGUEZ
Gomez who arrived from Habana on 27 November 1961. He carried
Diplomatic Passport Number 570 with Visa number D-407 issued in
Habana on 27 December 1961. His date and place of birth were
given as 11 March 1928, in Habana.
3. A usually reliable source, in a report dated 23 April 1962, stated
that Ramiro RODRIGUEZ Gomez was the First Secretary of the Cuban
Embassy in Rio de Janeiro.
4. An unknown source, in August 1962, reported that Ramiro RODRIGUEZ,
who arrived in Brazil on 28 December 1961, as First Secretary,
was formerly the First Secretary of the Cuban Embassy in Venezuela
and was identified as a G-2 agent there. He was born on
11 March 1928. He was Secretary General of the Match Industry
Workers' Union before 1961, at a time when it was in the hands
of the Communists.

RODRIGUEZ spurns her attempts at being chummy again. Source
feels that RODRIGUEZ is up to something special and notes
that he is in close contact with General Luiz Gonzaga LEITE,
the organizer of the Cuba Solidarity Congress. According
to RODRIGUEZ' file card in the Protocol Division of the 24
Ministry of Foreign Affairs RODRIGUEZ was born on 11 March 1928,
in Habana. His wife is IseT DOMINGUEZ de Armas and they
have one daughter, Eva RODRIGUEZ Dominquez, who was born
